Contemporary ceramic artist — born in Avellino, 1976

Angelica Tulimiero

My love for clay began in childhood, in my grandmother's ceramic studio in Naples — the colours in the jars, the warmth of the kiln, the tools laid out on the bench. Only now do I understand how deeply that room shaped the choices of my life. I trained in ceramics at the Istituto Statale d'Arte De Luca in Avellino, learning the Majolica tradition, before moving to London at the turn of the millennium to study ceramics, painting and design at Morley College, and to work as a studio assistant at Stoltzman & Thomas Sculptural Ceramics.

I am instinctively drawn to intricate surfaces. The more I look, the more I see that everything is made of patterns — matter has an inner structure, a world of repetitive forms. I hand-build in stoneware and porcelain, projecting myself into this fractal anatomy and following the guidance of nature, which offers an endless variety of textures and shapes. Each piece is unique, born from the encounter between material, form and gesture.

This search for pattern is what has drawn me across the world. Each place, each clay, each way of firing reveals surfaces I could find nowhere else: I have built wood kilns and fired in the United States, in Indonesia, and most recently in China, in residencies at Jingdezhen and Longquan. It was there that I came to celadon — the pale aquamarine glaze that has covered Asian porcelain for over a millennium — and to a way of holding light on a surface that has reshaped my work since.

That same restlessness keeps me experimenting closer to home. With the Ceramicoscopio collective in Nove I work with wood firing, and in France I collaborate with the CPIFAC ceramic centre. The work that emerges from this research has been shown and awarded internationally — from the first prize at l'Alcora to solo exhibitions in Venice and Amsterdam — though my studio remains rooted in Bozzolo, in northern Italy.

Alongside my studio practice I offer ceramics courses, workshops and open-studio sessions, welcoming anyone who wishes to approach clay, from beginners to advanced makers. I also collaborate with designers, architects and collectors on bespoke works for private and public spaces.
